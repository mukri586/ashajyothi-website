Chief Functionary · Secretary · Correspondent · Since 1998

"She started with two children and a borrowed room. Twenty-seven years later, she hasn't stopped."

Madhavi Latha Mareedu is the founder and driving force behind AshaJyothi Handicapped Welfare Society. A visionary social impact executive with 25+ years of distinguished service dedicated to advancing the welfare of women and children — particularly those with disabilities — she has built from nothing a permanent campus that is home to 70+ children who had nowhere else to go.

Her work has been instrumental in impacting 4,100+ women and children across Krishna and West Godavari Districts: rehabilitating over 1,000 children, educating 25,000+ girls on menstrual hygiene, distributing over a million sanitary pads through the PARI unit, and securing Stem Cell and surgical treatment for hundreds of children through international partnerships.

She holds an MSW and M.Sc. in Psychology, has served as a quasi-judicial Chairperson of the Child Welfare Committee under the JJ Act 2015, and has been recognised by the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, international humanitarian organisations, and the Government of India. She is still at the campus every day.

Professional experience/ 03

A career built in service.

Every role has been in service of children and women who needed someone to fight for them.

Chief Functionary / Secretary / Correspondent — AshaJyothi Handicapped Welfare Society

Hanuman Junction, Andhra Pradesh · June 1998 – Present (27+ Years)
I. Institutional Growth & Infrastructure Development
  • Pioneering Growth & Scale: Founded and scaled the organisation from 2 children to a comprehensive welfare society serving 100+ residential beneficiaries with special education, paramedical care, and 24/7 nursing.
  • Land Acquisition & Permanent Campus: Secured land and oversaw construction of a permanent, multi-facility campus with separate hostels, specialized therapy units, kitchen, and school — supported by international partner SDWH, Holland.
  • Operational Resilience: Developed critical infrastructure including specialized Hydrotherapy and Physio-rehabilitation rooms for in-house treatment, and ensured self-sufficiency via solar power installation and borewell systems for agriculture and water security.
II. Critical Healthcare & Rehabilitation Programs
  • Stem Cell Therapy Programme: Launched a pioneering partnership with Neurogen Brain and Spine Institute, successfully securing CM Relief Fund for 40 cases and facilitating Stem Cell Therapy for 50 children with severe neurological conditions.
  • Surgical Impact: Organised numerous medical camps resulting in over 100 major corrective surgeries — Polio, Achilles Tendon, Cleft Lip & Palate, and Cochlear Implants — with long-term post-operative care provided on campus.
  • ASRAM Hospital MOU: Established a formal MOU with ASRAM Hospital, Eluru, securing an exclusive ward and subsidised healthcare for orphan and semi-orphan children across the district.
  • Community Based Rehabilitation (CBR): Created a mobile CBR programme to extend active physiotherapy and support to more than 100 girl children in neighbouring villages who could not travel to campus.

Chairperson — Child Welfare Committee (CWC)

West Godavari District, Govt. of Andhra Pradesh · 2015 – 2019
  • Judicial Authority: Appointed to function as a Bench of First Class Judicial Magistrates under Section 27(9) of the JJ Act 2015, providing statutory care and protection for children in need (CNCP) across the district.
  • Child Care Institution Oversight: Mandated the monitoring and inspection of all Child Care Institutions (CCIs) in West Godavari District, ensuring adherence to constitutional standards of safety and welfare.
  • Welfare Initiatives: Orchestrated Half Saree functions for adolescent orphan girls as a dignified coming-of-age rite, and provided essential supplies including sweaters and sports kits to Government Children Homes.

Mandal Rehabilitation Supervisor

West Godavari District · 2002 – 2004
  • Mobilised 1,000+ women with disabilities to access government disability certificates, pensions, and welfare benefits — many of whom had never previously received any state support.
  • Recognised by the District Collector with the District Best Social Worker Award (2003) for the scale and quality of community outreach achieved during this tenure.
Social impact & advocacy/ 04

Beyond the campus gates.

AshaJyothi's work reaches far beyond the 70+ children who live there — into schools, villages, government offices, and disaster zones.

I

Women's Health & Economic Empowerment

Dignity for girls. Independence for women. Built at scale.

  • PARI Sanitary Pads Initiative: Conceptualized and executed the PARI Manufacturing Unit as a Women Empowerment and "Save the Girl Child" project. The unit has distributed over one million sanitary pads, generating self-employment for local women and making hygiene accessible across the region.
  • Menstrual Hygiene Advocacy: Personally addressed hundreds of girls' schools and colleges, educating 25,000+ girls across Andhra Pradesh and Telangana to dismantle the cultural taboo around menstruation — one classroom at a time.
  • Self-Help Groups (SHGs): Directly facilitated the formation of five Self-Help Groups for 50 women with disabilities, successfully connecting them with micro-loans, skill training, and sustainable livelihood pathways.
II

Inclusive Education & Rehabilitation Reform

Every child in school. No exceptions.

  • 100% Enrollment Campaign: As an IED Teacher, led intensive door-to-door campaigns that achieved 100% school enrollment and directly mainstreamed 145 children with disabilities into regular schools — recognised nationally by Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan.
  • Chinnari Choopu Programme: Managed this large-scale early identification initiative where 5,000 children were screened for developmental conditions, resulting in 100 referrals for specialised treatment and surgical care.
  • Dropout Prevention: Secured sponsorships and targeted assistance for 5,000+ underprivileged children across the district to prevent school dropout — ensuring education was not surrendered to poverty.
  • Home-Based Therapy: Pioneered low-cost, family-centred rehabilitation by motivating and training parents to install 15 parallel bars and special comprehension boards at home, enabling continuous therapeutic practice between campus visits.
III

Disaster & Crisis Management

When the state was overwhelmed, AshaJyothi showed up.

  • Hudhud Cyclone Relief (2014): Led critical relief operations in the aftermath of Cyclone Hudhud — coordinating the rebuilding of 50 homes and the distribution of 20,000+ meals to displaced and affected communities.
  • Covid-19 Response (2020–21): Provided dry ration kits to thousands of migrating workers stranded during lockdown, and supplied cots, beds, and Oxygen Concentrators to 4 Isolation Centres established by the Andhra Pradesh Health Department.
  • Flood Relief: Actively participated in flash flood relief across Eluru, NTR District, and the Kolleru Lake area — distributing essential goods, clothing, and medical supplies to stranded families and those rendered homeless.
