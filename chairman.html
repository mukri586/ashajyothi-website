Chairman · AshaJyothi

Madhavi
Latha
Mareedu

Chief Functionary · Secretary · Correspondent · Since 1998

"She started with two children and a borrowed room. Twenty-seven years later, she hasn't stopped."

Madhavi Latha Mareedu is the founder and driving force behind AshaJyothi Handicapped Welfare Society. Born in 1979, she has spent her entire adult life in service — building, from nothing, a permanent campus that is home to 70+ children with severe disabilities who had nowhere else to go.

Her work spans medical rehabilitation, inclusive education, disaster relief, women's empowerment, and judicial child welfare. She holds an MSW and M.Sc. in Psychology, has served as a quasi-judicial Chairperson of the Child Welfare Committee under the JJ Act 2015, and has been recognised by the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, international humanitarian organisations, and the Government of India.

She is still at the campus every day.
