Warm greetings from AshaJyothi

AshaJyothi Handicapped Welfare Society was born from difficulties and agony faced by our own family in the year 1998. This is a story of how one man turned his life's tragedy into a blessing for many. The story of a father who lost his daughter "BalaJyothi" at a young age who suffered from mental illness. He honored her memory by starting AshaJyothi to save children with special needs, orphan, abandoned and underprivileged.

Children who are physically challenged, speech and hearing impaired, Autistic, Downs Syndrome and Blind. Children who cannot walk, eat, bathe. Children who cannot accomplish basic tasks in life that we often take for granted. Children who need love, security, medical care, and necessities of life. All of them were either abandoned or orphaned at an incredibly young age. It is disheartening to see such children being left out and pathetic to see parents leaving such children at orphanages or abandoning treating them as a liability. This is the exact reason why AshaJyothi was founded with the objective to rescue and rehabilitate such children.

AshaJyothi began its journey with a small home to protect mentally disabled and abandoned children at Hanuman Junction, West Godavari District, India in 1998 with 10 children.

In the year 2005 AshaJyothi shifted to its own premises. Thanks a ton, to our sponsors "Care n Share", and others who provided relentless support in realizing our dreams. They helped us in the process to build our own home and comfortable premises with all basic amenities like beds, toilets, health care, kitchen, school and para medical support. AshaJyothi campus now helps more than 80 special needs children.

With the support of Neurogen, Mumbai we were able to provide the latest medical treatment for our children. We have well-equipped in-house facility for physiotherapy and 24/7 nursing support. Our children need continuous medical evaluation, physiotherapy, aids and appliances for the physically challenged like wheelchairs, hearing-aids, etc. and special education & training for survival.

We, at AshaJyothi not only work to make the children normal, but also teach them the required skills to empower them to live on their own in future. Thanks to the volunteers from Ireland, Germany etc., who visit us every year and provide various therapies to our children.

We sincerely acknowledge the support from various other organizations like Save the Child Foundation, Excel n Empower for their continued support to AshaJyothi.

Our mission is only to see Smiles on the faces of special needs children and that such smiles should sustain for lifetime. We appeal to individuals, Organizations and Philanthropists to support us to accomplish this mission. This will only enable AshaJyothi to extend all its services to protect these children.

Sincerely
Madhavi Latha

Mission

AshaJyothi Handicapped Welfare Society is a registered Voluntary Organization started in 1998 in Hanuman Junction, Andhra Pradesh, India. The Mission of AshaJyothi is to provide diverse services for development, education and rehabilitation for children with physical and mental disabilities. AshaJyothi strongly believes that these abandoned children with disabilities and special needs have the natural right to live in a home the gives them love and affection, healthy food, decent shelter, access to medical and health care facilities, not the least appropriate education to sustain.

Treatments at AshaJyothi

Cerebral Palsy

Cerebral Palsy is a non progressive neurodevelopmental disorder, which is caused by any event leading to damage to the brain around the time of birth. The causes can range from to nutritional factors, physical trauma, hypoxia / lack of Oxygen flow due to factors such as cord around the neck, to birth asphyxia etc. The impact ranges from mild physical disability to very severe disability along with comorbid mental retardation. Some Children also have accompanied fits/ Seizures / Convulsions, that complicate the Prognosis.

Autism

Autism is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by impaired by impaired social interaction, verbal, and non verbal communication and restricted and repetitive behavior.

Mental Retardation/ Intellectual Disability

Mental Retardation is a developmental disorder. Children with ID generally exhibit sub average functioning. These kids have difficulties in self adaptive areas such as communication, self care, home living, social skills, community use, self direction, health and safety, academics, leisure and work. Mental Retardation can be classified into the following grades according to its severity, viz. Mild, moderate, sever, and profound.

Down Syndrome

Down Syndrome is a genetic condition also known as 'Trisomy21' as it is caused due to the presence of an extra copy of chromosome 21. It leads to delayed physical growth, facial dysmorphism, and affected cognitive and intellectual functions.

Physically Challenged

Someone who is physically challenged has a problem with their body that makes it difficult for them to do things that other people can do easily. Children who are physically challenged because of Polio, accidents and other neurological problems.
